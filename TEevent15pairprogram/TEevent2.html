Competitive Pair Programming

Competitive Pair Programming is an event where two participants work together to solve a programming problem, each taking on specific roles. The competition emphasizes teamwork, communication, and collaboration while coding.

Rules:

1. Team of Two: Participants must work in pairs. One is the Driver (who writes the code) and the other is the Navigator (who guides and reviews the code).

2. Role Switch: Roles must be switched at regular intervals (e.g., every 10-15 minutes). Teams can have multiple role switches depending on the length of the challenge. 3. Time Limit: The total time to solve the problem

4. Problem Complexity: The problems are designed to require collaborationâ€”such as algorithms, data structures, or solving real world coding challenges (e.g., building a small feature or application). 5. No Solo Work: The Navigator cannot touch the keyboard, and the Driver cannot make significant decisions alone. Every move must be discussed. 6. Communication Focus: Teams are judged on how well they communicate and collaborate, not just on the correctness of the solution.

7. Scoring Criteria: Code Quality: Well-structured, readable, and functional code is a priority. Problem Solving: How efficiently the problem is solved and whether all parts of the problem are addressed.
