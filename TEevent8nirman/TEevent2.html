Nirman

Nirman-AutoCAD competition is an exciting way to introduce them to practical design and drafting skills.

1. Competition Format Individual or Team-Based:

Individual or Team-Based: Depending on the difficulty level, you can have individual participation or teams of 2-3 students. Time Limit: Set a reasonable time limit, like 1-2 hours, to complete the design task. Stages: o Preliminary Round: A simpler task for the first filtering. o Final Round: A more challenging and complex design problem for selected participants.

2. Task Ideas • Simple 2D Drafting (Prelims):

o Drawing basic mechanical or architectural designs like a gear, a floor plan, or a simple part. o Focusing on precision, correct usage of dimensions, and speed. • Complex 2D or 3D Modelling (Finals): o Advanced drafting tasks, such as designing a mechanical component, 3D modeling of a simple product, or an architectural model of a room/building. o Focus on layers, dimensions, and accuracy in 2D, or proper rendering and detailing in 3D models.

3. Judging Criteria

• Accuracy: Proper scaling, dimensioning, and use of AutoCAD commands. • Creativity: For design-related tasks, assess creativity in applying concepts. • Time Management: Completing the task within the given time. • Technical Skills: Proficiency with AutoCAD tools like layers, blocks, views, and annotations.
